# Hallmarks of Cancer

The concept of "Hallmarks of Cancer" describes the fundamental biological capabilities acquired by cancer cells during tumor development.

This framework was introduced to explain how diverse genetic mutations observed in tumors ultimately converge into a limited number of functional changes that allow cancer cells to survive, proliferate, and spread.

Understanding these hallmarks provides a unifying framework for studying cancer biology and developing therapeutic strategies.

---

# The Original Hallmarks

The original framework proposed six essential capabilities that cancer cells acquire.

## 1. Sustaining Proliferative Signaling

Normal cells require external signals to grow and divide.

Cancer cells develop the ability to continuously activate growth signals, allowing them to proliferate without normal regulatory control.

This can occur through:

- overexpression of growth factor receptors
- mutations in signaling pathways
- autocrine signaling loops

---

## 2. Evading Growth Suppressors

Healthy tissues contain regulatory mechanisms that prevent uncontrolled cell division.

Cancer cells disable these growth-inhibitory pathways, often by inactivating tumor suppressor genes such as those responsible for controlling the cell cycle.

---

## 3. Resisting Cell Death

Cells possess internal mechanisms that trigger programmed cell death when damage occurs.

Cancer cells develop the ability to avoid these death signals, allowing abnormal cells to survive longer than they should.

This resistance to apoptosis is a key factor in tumor survival.

---

## 4. Enabling Replicative Immortality

Normal cells have a limited number of divisions due to telomere shortening.

Cancer cells activate mechanisms that maintain telomere length, allowing them to divide indefinitely.

This capability enables tumors to grow continuously.

---

## 5. Inducing Angiogenesis

As tumors grow, they require oxygen and nutrients.

Cancer cells stimulate the formation of new blood vessels through a process called angiogenesis.

These new blood vessels supply the tumor with the resources required for continued growth.

---

## 6. Activating Invasion and Metastasis

One of the most dangerous properties of cancer is the ability to spread to distant organs.

Cancer cells acquire mechanisms that allow them to:

- detach from the primary tumor
- invade surrounding tissues
- enter the bloodstream
- colonize distant organs

This process is known as metastasis.

---

# Expanded Hallmarks

Later research expanded the framework by adding additional hallmarks and enabling characteristics.

These include:

## Avoiding Immune Destruction

Cancer cells develop strategies to escape recognition by the immune system.

This hallmark is particularly relevant for immunotherapy approaches such as CAR-T cell therapy.

---

## Tumor-Promoting Inflammation

Inflammatory processes within the tumor microenvironment can promote cancer progression by supporting cell proliferation and survival.

---

# Enabling Characteristics

Two additional factors support the acquisition of these hallmarks:

## Genome Instability

Cancer cells accumulate mutations at a higher rate than normal cells, accelerating tumor evolution.

---

## Tumor Microenvironment

Tumors interact with surrounding cells such as immune cells, fibroblasts, and blood vessels.

This microenvironment strongly influences tumor behavior and therapy response.

---

# Relevance for Immunotherapy

The hallmarks framework helps explain why cancer cells are difficult to eliminate.

Many therapies aim to disrupt one or more hallmarks.

Immunotherapy approaches, including CAR-T therapy, focus on enabling immune cells to recognize and eliminate tumor cells that have evolved mechanisms to evade immune detection.

Understanding the hallmarks of cancer provides a conceptual foundation for developing new therapeutic strategies.
